Citrus - Plant health app

Empower your citrus farming with a click.

Citrus, an innovative application powered by artificial intelligence, is designed to revolutionize citrus crop protection by streamlining the workflow, reducing operational inefficiencies, and cutting costs for organizations. Its unique feature enables farmers and inspectors to photograph crop anomalies, after which the app diagnoses the issue and provides immediate treatment recommendations, thereby empowering even novice inspectors or farmers to swiftly and accurately generate professional plant protection treatment plans. Additionally, the Citrus App is seamlessly integrated with a specialized e-commerce platform for Citrus products, including fertilizers, facilitating users to conveniently purchase necessary products directly through the online platform.

Literature Survey

Machine learning (ML) has revolutionized agricultural practices, particularly in disease detection for citrus crops. Traditional methods, like visual inspection, are often unreliable and inefficient. In contrast, ML models trained on images can detect diseases early and accurately. Studies have shown the effectiveness of ML in this domain. For instance, research [1] demonstrated that the EfficientNet-b0 model achieved a 99% accuracy and 98.6% F1 score in detecting citrus pests from 20,000 images. Another study [2] introduced a two-stage deep convolutional neural network (CNN) that distinguished between three citrus diseases with high accuracy, proving its value as a decision-support tool. Further, research [3] presented a deep learning model that achieved over 96% accuracy in disease detection, highlighting the potential to modernize agriculture. Hadipour-Rokni [4] focused on early detection of the Mediterranean fruit fly using a CNN-based system, emphasizing the synergy of machine vision and CNNs. Poonam Dhiman et al. [6] developed an edge-computing CNN-LSTM model that outperformed traditional models in efficiency and accuracy. Our research builds on these findings, aiming to use advanced ML algorithms like Roboflow and YOLOv8 for citrus disease identification. By deploying these on Google Colab and integrating with a Flutter application, we seek to create a system that accurately identifies leaf and fruit diseases, as well as pests. This could significantly reduce the impact of diseases on crop health and yield, and potentially transform agriculture across various crops. While ML offers more accurate and faster detection than traditional methods, challenges remain, such as annotating pest locations and the need for large datasets. Despite these limitations, the ongoing development of ML models is crucial for improving agricultural practices and food security.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Adaptive Learning in Disease Diagnosis:

Current systems for citrus disease diagnosis rely heavily on user input and predefined knowledge bases, which can limit their ability to adapt to new or unrecorded disease variants. There’s a need for more dynamic models that learn and evolve with emerging data, reducing dependency on static databases.

Real-Time Pest Management:

While existing applications provide valuable information on citrus pests, they lack real-time interaction and decision-making capabilities. Integrating real-time data analysis and automated control recommendations could significantly enhance pest management strategies and crop health monitoring.

Automated Pest Identification:

The reliance on traditional methods and manual image analysis in pest identification apps limits their efficiency. Incorporating machine learning algorithms for automated image recognition could streamline the identification process, providing faster and more accurate pest detection and management solutions.

Research Problem & Solution

Problem:

How can the implementation of a intelligent citrus farm monitoring system improve the early detection and classification of citrus disease diagnosis, pest identification, and nutrient deficiency detection, compared to traditional methods reliant on human visual inspection focusing on the health of citrus plants, including leaves, fruits, and the impact of nutrient levels, and what impact does this have on the efficiency, sustainability, and overall yield in citrus farming?

Solution:

Our solution aims to revolutionize citrus farm management by developing an intelligent monitoring system that leverages the YOLOv8 object detection model for efficient disease and pest detection. This automated system enables early disease detection and precise classification, significantly outperforming human visual inspection. Enhanced with deep machine learning technologies, the system offers real-time observation capabilities without requiring physical presence. It includes a user-friendly app for disease identification through image upload, providing organic and biological treatment recommendations. Additionally, the system maps pest spread in the field, offering valuable insights for effective pest management. This innovative approach is expected to significantly improve operational efficiency and sustainability, contribute to global food security, and prevent economic losses for growers and consumers by effectively identifying and managing issues such as diseases like canker, greening, and blackspot; pests like the citrus fruit piercing moth, lemon butterfly, and fruit fly; and nutritional disorders like potassium, nitrogen, and phosphorus deficiencies.

Research Objectives

Intelligent Disease and Pest Detection:

Utilizing the advanced YOLOv8 object detection model, our system introduces a groundbreaking method for early detection and precise classification of citrus diseases and pests. By surpassing the limitations of human visual inspection, it provides real-time monitoring and accurate diagnostics, ensuring immediate and effective responses to threats. This proactive approach minimizes crop damage and maximizes yield quality, establishing a new standard in agricultural health management.

User-Friendly Diagnostic Application:

At the core of our solution is a user-centric application that simplifies disease identification. Farmers can upload images of affected crops, receive instant analysis, and obtain tailored organic and biological treatment plans. This empowers growers with actionable insights, enabling them to address issues swiftly and sustainably. The app's intuitive design ensures accessibility for users of all technological backgrounds, fostering widespread adoption and enhancing the overall impact of the monitoring system.

Pest Spread Mapping and Management:

Our innovative system not only detects pests but also tracks their spread across fields using sophisticated mapping technology. By visualizing pest distribution, farmers can implement strategic interventions to control infestations. This data-driven approach optimizes resource allocation, reduces unnecessary pesticide use, and promotes an eco-friendly farming environment. The resulting insights contribute to a holistic pest management strategy that aligns with environmental conservation efforts.

Heatmap Visualization for Targeted Interventions:

The system's ability to generate heatmaps based on geographical data of disease occurrences revolutionizes intervention strategies. By providing a clear visual representation of disease hotspots, it enables farmers and agronomists to concentrate their efforts where they are most needed. This targeted approach ensures efficient use of resources, enhances the effectiveness of treatments, and supports the long-term health and productivity of citrus orchards.

Methodology

Figure :. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed solution provides a comprehensive application aimed at addressing the prevalent issue of citrus diseases through a multi-faceted methodology that leverages modern technology. The architecture of our approach consists of several key components, each contributing to the system's overall efficiency and effectiveness.

1.Image Capture with Flutter Application:

The process begins with a user-friendly Flutter application designed for farmers to capture high-quality images of citrus plants. The app's intuitive interface ensures ease of use, encouraging farmers to regularly document their crops.

2.Image Transmission to Backend System:

Once captured, the images are automatically transmitted to our backend system. This transfer occurs seamlessly and securely, ensuring that data integrity is maintained throughout the process.

3.Processing with YOLOv8 Model:

The core of our backend system is powered by the YOLOv8 model, a state-of-the-art machine learning model renowned for its accuracy and speed in object detection tasks. Upon receiving the images, YOLOv8 processes them to identify the presence of diseases. This model's advanced algorithms enable it to detect even subtle signs of disease that might be missed by the human eye.

4.Treatment Recommendations:

Based on the detected diseases, the system then suggests appropriate organic and biological treatments. These recommendations are tailored to the specific conditions identified, providing farmers with actionable guidance to mitigate the issues effectively.

5.Geospatial Analysis and Heatmap Generation:

Our system also integrates geospatial analysis to enhance disease management further. By leveraging the geographical location data associated with each image, the system generates heatmaps that visually represent disease prevalence across different regions. This heatmap functionality is crucial for understanding patterns and trends in disease outbreaks, allowing for more targeted and effective interventions.
